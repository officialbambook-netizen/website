The science

Treated water is not beauty-clean water.

Municipal water is treated for safety — not designed for your hair. Here's what research on shower water has confirmed is present:1

  • Chlorine & chloramines — the disinfectants that stay in the water when it reaches your shower
  • Sediment & rust particles — physical debris shed from ageing infrastructure
  • Inorganic salts & microbes — additional residual contaminants in distribution systems
  • Pipe residue — rust, copper, and trace metals from pipes that are often decades old
  • Trihalomethanes — chemical byproducts formed when chlorine reacts with organic matter in the supply

1 National Institutes of Health, PMC2874882

For your hair

Your products may be working against the wrong baseline.

Your hair routine doesn't begin when you open the shampoo. It begins when water hits your hair. Chlorine strips the natural protective film your hair depends on — before anything else has a chance to work.

For most hair this is a small cumulative stressor, not a single event. Repeated contact before every product you use. It keeps accumulating every shower.

For your colour

Your stylist did their job. Your shower keeps getting a vote.

Colour-treated hair is chemistry. Chlorine is an oxidiser. Every rinse is contact time between your colour and your water.

Purple shampoo corrects what already shifted. It doesn't change your ruined shaft or your oxidized color. You spend $200–$300 on the colour every 6–10 weeks — your shower works against it every morning.

For your skin

Your skin routine starts after your shower. Your skin barrier does not.

Hair is the most visible impact. Skin is the quieter one.

Research on bathing water found that free residual chlorine reduces the skin's water-holding capacity — the outer layer responsible for retaining moisture.2 Your barrier cream and ceramide serum are working in recovery mode, not maintenance.
