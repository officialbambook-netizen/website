OVERVIEW

Lavero ("we", "us", "our") is committed to maintaining your trust by respecting and protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy describes why and how we collect, use, share, and protect personal information collected from and about visitors and customers of our website. It also explains your choices regarding how we use your personal information and communicate with you, how you can request access to and correction of your data, and other important considerations. By visiting our website and/or purchasing our products, you agree to the practices described in this Privacy Policy. In order to protect your personal data when you shop at our website, we provide this Privacy Policy, which is a contract governing your use of our services.

SECTION 1 – INFORMATION WE COLLECT

Personal information means any information relating to an identified or identifiable person. Personal information does not include data that has been irreversibly anonymized or aggregated so that it can no longer identify you individually. We collect and process the following categories of personal information.

Identity and contact data: your name, email address, shipping and billing address, phone number, and country of residence. We collect this information when you create an account, place an order, or otherwise interact with us, and it is necessary to process and fulfill your orders.

Transaction and payment data: the products you purchase, your order history, order value, delivery details, and limited payment information such as payment method type. We do not store full payment card numbers. Full card data is collected and processed by our payment service providers in accordance with applicable PCI-DSS security standards.

Technical data: your IP address, browser type and version, device identifiers, operating system, time zone and location, and referring URLs. This information is collected automatically when you visit our website.

Usage data: pages viewed, products browsed, items added to cart, time spent on site, click paths, and other interactions with our website. This information is collected automatically and helps us understand how our website is used.

Marketing and communications data: your preferences in receiving marketing from us and your communication preferences, including whether you have opted in to receive marketing emails from us.

Customer support data: the content of emails, messages, reviews, and other communications you send to us, including any information you choose to share about your product experience or a reaction to a product.

We may collect and store information locally on your device using mechanisms such as browser web storage, including HTML5, and application data caches. This information is collected through cookies and similar tracking technologies, which are described in Section 5.

We use the information we collect to provide, improve, and develop our products and services, to communicate with you, and to protect us and our customers. More specifically, we use your personal information for the following purposes.

We use your personal information to process and fulfill your orders, arrange delivery, handle returns and refunds, and manage your account. Without this information we would be unable to complete your purchase or arrange delivery of your products. We use it to communicate with you about your order, inform you about changes to our policies and terms, and respond to your support requests — we may use your email address, phone number, or shipping address to do this depending on the nature of the communication. We use it to measure the effectiveness of our advertising campaigns, including through advertising tools such as Meta Pixel, which helps us understand which of our ads are reaching the right people and driving purchases so that we can focus our marketing resources effectively.

SECTION 5 – COOKIES AND TRACKING TECHNOLOGIES

Our website uses cookies and similar technologies — including pixels, tags, and local storage — to operate our site, understand how it is used, and support our advertising. When you visit our website, the following categories of technology are active.

Strictly necessary cookies are required for the fundamental operation of our website, such as maintaining your session and shopping cart. These include session identifiers and cart tokens that allow the site to remember who you are as you navigate between pages. These cookies are always active and cannot be disabled without affecting your ability to use the site.

Functionality and preferences cookies remember your settings and preferences, such as your selected currency or region, so that we can provide a more personalized and convenient experience. If you disable these cookies, some features and saved preferences may not persist between visits.

Performance and analytics cookies, including Shopify Analytics, collect information about how visitors use our site — such as which pages are visited most often, how long visitors stay, and how they navigate between pages. This data is aggregated and used to improve the website experience. If you disable these cookies, we may not be able to measure and improve our service as effectively.

Advertising and targeting cookies, including Meta Pixel, collect information about your browsing activity across websites in order to deliver and measure targeted advertising on platforms such as Facebook and Instagram. These cookies may remember the websites you have visited and share that information with advertising partners who use it to show you ads relevant to your interests. If you opt out of these cookies, you may still see advertising from us but it will not be personalized based on your browsing history.

Social networking cookies may be active where our site includes content from or links to social media platforms. These cookies can enable you to share pages and content through third-party social platforms and may also be used for advertising purposes. If you reject these cookies, certain social sharing features may not be available.

You can opt out or manage your preferences in the following ways: you may block or delete cookies through your browser settings, though doing so may affect your ability to use certain features of our website; you may opt out of Meta advertising at facebook.com/settings/ads; and if your browser sends a Global Privacy Control (GPC) opt-out preference signal, we will treat this as a request to opt out of data sharing for targeted advertising purposes for the device and browser you used to visit our site.

SECTION 7 – YOUR PRIVACY RIGHTS

We take reasonable steps to ensure that your personal data is accurate, complete, and up to date. Depending on where you live, you may have the following rights in relation to your personal information.

Right of access: you have the right to request confirmation of whether we process your personal data and, where we do, to obtain a copy of the personal data we hold about you together with information about how and why we process it. We will provide this information within 30 days of receiving your request.

Right to rectification: you have the right to request that we correct any inaccurate personal data we hold about you, or to complete any incomplete personal data, including by providing a supplementary statement. We will make corrections promptly and will inform you when the update has been made.

Right to erasure: you have the right to request that we delete your personal data in certain circumstances — for example, where the data is no longer necessary for the purposes for which it was collected, or where you withdraw your consent and there is no other lawful basis for processing. Please note that we may not be able to erase data that we are required to retain to comply with a legal obligation or to fulfill a contract with you.

Right to restrict processing: you have the right to request that we limit how we process your personal data in certain circumstances — for example, where you contest the accuracy of the data and we need time to verify it, or where you have objected to processing and we are considering whether our legitimate grounds override your interests. While processing is restricted, we will continue to store your data but will not use it for other purposes.

Right to data portability: you have the right to receive the personal data you have provided to us in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format and, where technically feasible, to have that data transmitted directly to another controller. This right applies where the processing is based on your consent or on a contract with you, and is carried out by automated means.

Right to object: you have the right to object at any time to our processing of your personal data where we rely on legitimate interests as our legal basis. We will cease processing unless we can demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds that override your interests or unless we need the data to establish, exercise, or defend legal claims. You also have an absolute right to object to direct marketing at any time, and we will cease that processing immediately without requiring justification.

Rights related to automated decision-making: you have the right not to be subject to decisions based solely on automated processing, including profiling, that produce legal or similarly significant effects on you. We do not currently make final decisions with legal or similarly significant effects based solely on automated processing without human review — for example, our fraud detection tools flag transactions for human assessment rather than automatically refusing them.

Right to withdraw consent: where we rely on your consent as the legal basis for processing your personal data, you have the right to withdraw that consent at any time. Withdrawal of consent will not affect the lawfulness of any processing that took place before the withdrawal.

Right to lodge a complaint: if you believe we have not handled your personal data in accordance with applicable law, you have the right to lodge a complaint with a relevant supervisory authority. In the United States, you may contact the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) at ftc.gov or your state Attorney General's office. California residents may also contact the California Privacy Protection Agency at cppa.ca.gov. We would, however, appreciate the opportunity to address your concerns before you approach any authority — please contact us at support@mylavero.com in the first instance.

If you are a California resident, you have additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA/CPRA), including the right to know what personal information we collect and share, the right to request deletion of your data, the right to correct inaccurate data, the right to opt out of the sharing of your personal information for targeted advertising purposes, and the right to non-discrimination for exercising your rights. Residents of other states with applicable privacy laws, including Virginia, Colorado, Texas, and Connecticut, may have similar rights under their respective state laws. We will respond to all verified requests within 30 days and will not charge a fee unless your request is manifestly unfounded or excessive.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at support@mylavero.com.
